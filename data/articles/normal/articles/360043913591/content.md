<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There is now an easier way for you to identify if a community is right for you.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Discord is home to many servers and interesting user-interface designs ranging from the pound sign next to a channel (</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">#hash-tag</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">), a million names on the right side of the screen (</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Members- 99999</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">), and disabled chat bars (</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">please read the #rules first</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We understand this can be very overwhelming to users, so we have created a better user experience on first contact with these communities.</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Whether this is your first couple of servers you are joining, the 50</span></em><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">th</span></em><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or a Server Owner looking to simplify your community, we introduce to you the Community Server Welcome Screen.</span></em></p>

<h1 id="h_01EEJXDA1DTCAHTWEXMJF95TXG">How to Enable the Welcome Screen</h1>
<p>To enable the Welcome Screen, your server must have enabled the Community Server Settings! If you haven't done so yet, check out <a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047132851" target="_blank" rel="noopener">this support article</a> on how to do so! </p>
<h1 id="h_ee7fc27b-780e-4455-afc7-8737772c6a3a">Customization for Community Server Owners</h1>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Community Server Owners and those with </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Manage Server</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> permission now have access to this option, you can customize it under Server Settings.</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/360063309772/Screen_Shot_2020-07-31_at_10.04.35_AM.png" alt="Screen_Shot_2020-07-31_at_10.04.35_AM.png" width="509" height="405"></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">From here you can click on </span><strong>“Set Up Welcome Screen”</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to get started, you will then receive a window to </span><strong>“Add a Recommended Channel”</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Select any channel that you prefer users to see within your server and write a little description about it you want others to see.</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/360057682852/welcome_screen_add_a_recommended_channel.png" alt="welcome_screen_add_a_recommended_channel.png" width="587" height="377"></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Once you have saved your customization you will get an overview of what it will look like. From here, you can continue to add 4 more options or hit </span><strong>“Enable”</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to show it off to new, incoming users.</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/360058148152/welcome_screen_overview_with_channel.png" alt="welcome_screen_overview_with_channel.png" width="562" height="551"></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lastly, you can select </span><strong>“Preview”</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to get a visual on how it will look to new users to give you a better idea of their end.</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/9I5Ff_Af9nPS2XE5YjyUPVfgkkq8H9kpD6MfHKN77yO4w1laCObEUEpQwZhVwpX3kVZvNfHXZjG7XuUspFb9DaMgGx8c74yBlAi2KL5Z4-Dh_SgkC71PJox2iEUVJke1K62oivhr" width="550" height="701"><br><br>And that is it! Congratulations on setting up your Community Server Welcome Screen!</p>
<h2 id="h_e039037f-36e8-4afe-b478-48e157ee26f6"><strong>Recommended Channels Guide</strong></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now that you have an idea of how to set up your Welcome Screen, it can be a bit difficult to decide what channels to spotlight in your summary from your already amazing 100+ other <em>#text-channels.</em></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Here are some recommendations from us on what type of channels to add to your Welcome Screen.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span></p>
<ul>
    <li style="font-weight: 400;">
        <span style="font-weight: 400;">A channel where new users can interact (talk, chat, react) with others already within the server. (e.g. </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">#general, #discussion</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">)</span>
    </li>
    <li style="font-weight: 400;">
        <span style="font-weight: 400;">#</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rules </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">channel to make sure active engagement is safe for everyone in the community and within ToS/guidelines.</span>
    </li>
    <li style="font-weight: 400;">
        <span style="font-weight: 400;">An </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">#announcements</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> channel to get the latest news specific to your community activities.</span>
    </li>
    <li style="font-weight: 400;">
        <span style="font-weight: 400;">#</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Finding-Friends </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">or</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> #</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Looking-for-Group</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> channels for those that want to jump right into the action.</span>
    </li>
</ul>
<p id="h_617a4507-2b67-473a-882a-87834490f2df"><span style="font-weight: 400;">We also suggest naming these channels to ones that are easily definitive and understood by those within and outside of the community for extra clarity.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">By naming the channel after the action or activity that is to take place within that text/voice channel, this can prevent confusion on what users are allowed to do within each channel. (</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">e.g. #pictures-only, #meet-new-friends, #introduce-yourself)</span></em><strong><br></strong></p>
<h1 id="h_a3d4c6bd-1139-4363-b457-bdf591ea1275">
    <strong>Why Should I Set This Up?</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span>
</h1>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sometimes large communities with many channels, users, and large walls of text can be a bit too confusing for newcomers that want a clear direction of where to go.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">This can contribute to bad user experience and cause a user who would have contributed greatly to your community to get lost in our UI.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">To combat this, </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">the Welcome Screen provides ease of access and less friction for new Discord users and long time users alike. The impact generated from setting up a Welcome Screen will help with increased retention of users old and new, and raise engagement within your community. </span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">This means more people to stick around and engage with the community in a positive way!</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">We hope this helps you in your future endeavors in growing your user base by offering a bit more customization and simplicity.</span></p>
